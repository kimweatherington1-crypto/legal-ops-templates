# Matter Intake SOP — Litigation
**Kim Weatherington | Legal Operations Portfolio**

---

## Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) governs the intake and onboarding of new litigation matters from initial client contact through full file setup, deadline calendaring, and team assignment. Consistent execution of this SOP ensures no matter falls through the cracks, deadlines are captured immediately, and the assigned team has everything needed to begin work on Day 1.

---

## Scope
Applies to: Commercial Litigation · Construction Litigation · Business Disputes · Appellate Matters

---

## Step 1 — Initial Inquiry & Conflict Check

| Action | Owner | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Receive new matter inquiry (phone, email, referral) | Paralegal / Legal Ops | Same day |
| Document client name, opposing party, matter type, and key dates | Paralegal / Legal Ops | Same day |
| Run conflict check in practice management system (Clio / iManage) | Paralegal / Legal Ops | Within 2 hours |
| Report conflict check results to supervising attorney | Paralegal / Legal Ops | Before client engagement |
| If conflict identified — escalate immediately, do NOT proceed | Attorney | Immediate |

**⚠️ No client communication, engagement letter, or file creation until conflict check is CLEARED.**

---

## Step 2 — Client Intake & Engagement

- [ ] Send client intake form (secure link or DocuSign)
- [ ] Collect: full legal name, entity type, contact information, matter summary
- [ ] Prepare and send engagement/retainer letter for execution via DocuSign
- [ ] Confirm retainer receipt and record in billing system
- [ ] Obtain all existing documents, correspondence, and evidence from client
- [ ] Document original source and date received for each item

---

## Step 3 — File Setup

### Practice Management System (Clio / iManage)
- [ ] Create new matter file — naming convention: `[ClientLastName]_[MatterType]_[Year]`
- [ ] Assign matter number
- [ ] Enter all parties, contacts, and opposing counsel
- [ ] Set matter type, practice area, and billing arrangement
- [ ] Upload engagement letter and signed retainer
- [ ] Set file status to ACTIVE

### Physical / Digital File Structure
```
Matter Folder
├── 01_Pleadings
├── 02_Correspondence
├── 03_Discovery
│   ├── Requests Propounded
│   └── Responses Received
├── 04_Motions
├── 05_Research
├── 06_Court_Filings
├── 07_Depositions
├── 08_Expert_Witnesses
├── 09_Mediation_Arbitration
└── 10_Closing_Documents
```

## Step 4 — Deadline Calendaring

**Critical: All deadlines must be entered within 24 hours of matter intake.**

| Deadline Type | Calendar Entry Required | Reminder Settings |
|---|---|---|
| Statute of limitations | Yes — hard stop | 90 days · 60 days · 30 days · 7 days |
| Answer deadline | Yes | 14 days · 7 days · 3 days · Day before |
| Discovery cutoff | Yes | 60 days · 30 days · 14 days |
| Motion deadlines | Yes | 30 days · 14 days · 7 days |
| Mediation / arbitration dates | Yes | 30 days · 14 days · Day before |
| Court hearing dates | Yes | 30 days · 7 days · Day before |
| Trial date | Yes — hard stop | 90 days · 60 days · 30 days · 14 days |

**⚠️ Statute of limitations is a hard stop — set calendar reminders for attorney AND paralegal.**

---

## Step 5 — Team Assignment & Briefing

- [ ] Assign supervising attorney, paralegal, and any support staff
- [ ] Enter team assignments in matter management system
- [ ] Prepare matter summary memo for team briefing (see template below)
- [ ] Schedule team intake meeting within 48 hours of file opening
- [ ] Confirm each team member has system access to the matter file

### Matter Summary Memo — Template
```
MATTER SUMMARY
Date Opened:
Client:
Matter Type:
Opposing Party:
Opposing Counsel:
Court / Jurisdiction:
Case Number (if known):
Statute of Limitations:
Next Deadline:
Key Facts:
Client Objective:
Immediate Action Items:
```

---

## Step 6 — Initial Case Assessment

- [ ] Review all client-provided documents
- [ ] Identify and note key dates, parties, claims, and potential defenses
- [ ] Conduct preliminary legal research (Westlaw / LexisNexis)
- [ ] Prepare initial case assessment memo for attorney review
- [ ] Identify any urgent filings or immediate action required
- [ ] Confirm court e-filing registration and access for assigned jurisdiction

---

## Step 7 — File Audit (30 Days Post-Intake)

- [ ] Confirm all intake documents are uploaded and organized
- [ ] Verify all deadlines are calendared and accurate
- [ ] Confirm team assignments are current
- [ ] Update matter status in practice management system
- [ ] Flag any outstanding items to supervising attorney
